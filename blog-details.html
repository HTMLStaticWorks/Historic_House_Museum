The Secret Letters of Julia Sterling

Julian Black
March 15, 2026
12 Comments

It was during a routine audit of the Attic East wing that our team stumbled upon a false floorboard beneath the old mahogany desk. Inside, wrapped in silk, were over fifty letters written by Julia Sterling between 1888 and 1892.

These documents provide an unprecedented look into the personal life of the woman who essentially managed the estate's construction while her husband was traveling abroad. Julia wasn't just a benefactor; she was a meticulously organized project manager who challenged the architectural norms of the day.

"The stone arrived from the quarry today. It is not the shade of white I requested, but rather a soft cream. I believe, however, that it will catch the sunset even more beautifully than the white would have."

Julia Sterling, 1889

Architectural Challenges

One recurring theme in the letters is the difficulty of implementing the grand ballroom's vaulted ceiling. Julia's persistence with the engineers eventually led to the unique support structure that visitors admire today. Her letters detail the late-night meetings and the sheer force of will it took to see her vision completed.

We are currently in the process of digitizing these letters. Soon, members will be able to read the full transcripts as part of our Virtual Pass. This discovery reminds us that while the stones and wood of the estate are beautiful, it is the human stories within that give them their value.
