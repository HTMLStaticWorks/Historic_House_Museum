The Grand Estate Tour

  1. Tours
  2. Grand Estate

Witness the Heart of Heritage

The Grand Estate Tour is our most popular experience, providing a comprehensive walk through the architectural marvel that has stood for over a century.

Led by our expert curators, this tour covers the primary residence, including the legendary library with its 5,000 original volumes, the grand ballroom that hosted royalty, and the subterranean servants' quarters that offer a glimpse into the operational life of the estate.

What's Included:

  • 60-Minute Guided Walkthrough
  • Access to the Private Library & Map Room
  • Complimentary Heritage Brochure
  • Professional Photography Permitted

Historical Significance

The estate was designed in 1885 by Julian Sterling, whose work defined the regional architecture of the period. Every room tells a story of the family's contributions to the industry and the cultural shifts that occurred during the turn of the century.

Meet Your Curator

Dr. Alistair Thorne, Senior Resident Historian

"Our mission is to breathe life into these stones. Every artifact here isn't just a relic; it's a chapter of a story that continues to evolve with every visitor who walks through our doors."

Dr. Thorne has dedicated over 20 years to the preservation of 19th-century estates and leads the archival research for the Sterling family legacy.

Common Questions

To ensure the preservation of our collections, we ask that visitors refrain from touching any artifacts or historic furniture. However, we do have designated "touch history" stations where replicas can be handled.

We recommend comfortable walking shoes as the tour involves several flights of stairs and gravel paths. We ask that you avoid wearing sharp heels to protect our 19th-century hardwood flooring.
